About Heart Disease

Heart disease:

In comparison to the brain, which is the most important organ in the human body, the heart is the next most important organ. It circulates blood and supplies all of the body's organs. Heart disease ailments are the most common of all diseases.The term “heart disease” refers to several types of heart conditions. The most common type of heart disease in the United States is coronary artery disease (CAD), which affects the blood flow to the heart. Decreased blood flow can cause a heart attack.

What are the symptoms of heart disease?

Heart disease can sometimes be "silent," with no indications or symptoms until a person has a heart attack, heart failure, or an arrhythmia. Symptoms that occur as a result of these events include

  • Heart attack:Chest pain or discomfort, upper back or neck pain, indigestion, heartburn, nausea or vomiting, excessive weariness, upper body discomfort, disorientation, and shortness of breath are some of the symptoms that people experience.
  • Arrhythmia: Fluttering feelings in the chest (palpitations).
  • Heart failure: Shortness of breath, fatigue, or swelling of the feet, ankles, legs, abdomen, or neck veins.

The risk factors for heart disease:

Heart disease is exacerbated by high blood pressure, high cholesterol, and smoking. In the United States, nearly half of the population (47 percent) has at least one of these three risk factors. Heart disease can also be exacerbated by a variety of medical problems and lifestyle decisions, including

  • Diabetes
  • Overweight and obesity
  • Unhealthy diet
  • Physical inactivity
  • Excessive alcohol use

Cardiac rehabilitation:

Anyone recuperating from a heart attack, heart failure, or some forms of heart surgery should consider cardiac rehabilitation (rehab). Cardiac rehab is a supervised programme that incorporates a variety of activities.

  • Physical activity
  • Education about healthy living, including healthy eating, taking medicine as prescribed, and ways to help you quit smoking
  • Counseling to find ways to relieve stress and improve mental health

Your health care team, exercise and nutrition specialists, physical therapists, and counsellors or mental health professionals may all be able to assist you during cardiac rehab.
